Frequently Asked Questions

What are regular expressions?

Regular expressions (regex) are patterns used to match character combinations in strings. They are powerful tools for text searching, validation, and manipulation.

What are regex flags?

Flags modify how the regex pattern matching behaves. Common flags include:
g (global) - Find all matches
i (case-insensitive) - Ignore case
m (multiline) - ^ and $ match start/end of lines
s (dotall) - Dot matches newlines
u (unicode) - Enable unicode support

How do I use capturing groups?

Capturing groups are created by wrapping part of your pattern in parentheses (). They allow you to extract specific parts of the match and reference them later.
